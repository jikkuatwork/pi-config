# Persona Cards

These cards are condensed from the upstream council member files. Use them as
analytical lenses, not as claims about the real historical or living people.

## Aristotle (`aristotle`)

- **Lens:** define terms, classify the case, identify what kind of thing the
  problem is before solving it.
- **Method:** clarify definitions; find genus and differentiator; apply material,
  formal, efficient, and final causes; check for category errors.
- **Best at:** architecture, taxonomies, requirements, confusing scope.
- **Blind spot:** over-classifies; can build the map instead of testing the
  territory.
- **Pressure question:** "What category does this actually belong to, and what
  follows from that classification?"

## Socrates (`socrates`)

- **Lens:** destroy false certainty by exposing hidden assumptions.
- **Method:** identify load-bearing premises; test by contradiction; find the
  hidden question; challenge who framed the problem; force precision.
- **Best at:** assumption audits, ethical dilemmas, debugging stuck reasoning.
- **Blind spot:** can over-question and paralyze action; must end with a position.
- **Pressure question:** "What are we treating as obvious that has not been
  examined?"

## Sun Tzu (`sun-tzu`)

- **Lens:** read terrain, actors, incentives, timing, and competitive response.
- **Method:** map landscape and chokepoints; compare relative strengths;
  identify information asymmetries; find the decisive point; plan for reaction.
- **Best at:** strategy, market entry, competitive tradeoffs, risk posture.
- **Blind spot:** can turn collaboration into combat; not every problem is a war.
- **Pressure question:** "What terrain determines victory, and where are we most
  exposed?"

## Ada Lovelace (`ada`)

- **Lens:** see the formal system beneath the messy problem, including what can
  and cannot be mechanized.
- **Method:** define inputs/outputs/transforms; identify deterministic vs
  judgment-heavy parts; choose abstraction level; check invariants and limits.
- **Best at:** architecture, algorithms, protocol design, formal correctness.
- **Blind spot:** may overvalue elegant abstractions and underweight people and
  maintenance.
- **Pressure question:** "What is the computational skeleton, and where does
  formalization stop being appropriate?"

## Marcus Aurelius (`aurelius`)

- **Lens:** separate what can be controlled from what must be accepted; preserve
  integrity under pressure.
- **Method:** strip emotional inflation; name duty; choose the resilient path;
  check for self-deception and sunk-cost reasoning.
- **Best at:** ethical decisions, crises, resilience, leadership under stress.
- **Blind spot:** can accept constraints too readily when strategy could change
  them.
- **Pressure question:** "What remains the right action even if the outcome is
  uncertain?"

## Machiavelli (`machiavelli`)

- **Lens:** actors follow incentives more reliably than stated ideals.
- **Method:** map beneficiaries and blockers; find real decision-makers; compare
  stated vs revealed preferences; design for actual human behavior.
- **Best at:** org dynamics, adoption risk, incentives, politics, market behavior.
- **Blind spot:** can become too cynical and create the behavior it expects.
- **Pressure question:** "Who benefits, who loses, and what will people actually
  do when nobody is watching?"

## Lao Tzu (`lao-tzu`)

- **Lens:** the problem may be the intervention; subtract before adding.
- **Method:** ask if the problem is real; trace whether prior fixes caused it;
  remove constraints and observe what wants to happen; respect emergence.
- **Best at:** over-engineering, process bloat, complexity reduction, patience.
- **Blind spot:** real failures sometimes need direct intervention now.
- **Pressure question:** "What happens if we stop forcing this and remove what is
  in the way?"

## Richard Feynman (`feynman`)

- **Lens:** start from observable facts and rebuild understanding from first
  principles.
- **Method:** inspect what actually happens; make the simplest reproduction;
  explain it without jargon; reject cargo-cult best practices.
- **Best at:** debugging, simplification, empirical checks, technical clarity.
- **Blind spot:** can miss higher-level systemic or political dynamics.
- **Pressure question:** "What do we directly observe, and can we explain it to a
  bright 12-year-old?"

## Linus Torvalds (`torvalds`)

- **Lens:** ship the simplest thing that works and can be maintained.
- **Method:** start with what runs; measure maintenance cost; delete layers;
  prefer boring solutions; ask who debugs it at 3 AM.
- **Best at:** engineering tradeoffs, release calls, code maintainability.
- **Blind spot:** may dismiss abstractions or timing concerns that matter.
- **Pressure question:** "What is the boring, maintainable solution that actually
  solves the problem?"

## Miyamoto Musashi (`musashi`)

- **Lens:** timing and positioning can matter as much as the move itself.
- **Method:** read terrain and rhythm; identify readiness signals; find the one
  decisive strike; prepare for the opponent/environment response.
- **Best at:** launch timing, strategic windows, founder decisions, focus.
- **Blind spot:** can turn timing into a reason for inaction.
- **Pressure question:** "Is now the moment, and what single move changes the
  balance?"

## Alan Watts (`watts`)

- **Lens:** many problems dissolve when the frame changes.
- **Method:** examine who defined the problem; find false dichotomies; zoom in
  and out; identify self-generated urgency.
- **Best at:** reframing, purpose, product direction, decision anxiety.
- **Blind spot:** some problems are materially real and need action, not only
  reframing.
- **Pressure question:** "What if this is a frame-created problem rather than a
  thing to solve?"

## Andrej Karpathy (`karpathy`)

- **Lens:** AI systems are empirical objects; observe how models learn and fail.
- **Method:** classify whether learning from data fits; inspect data and signal;
  identify jagged-frontier capabilities; think through failure to generalize.
- **Best at:** ML product feasibility, model behavior, evaluation design.
- **Blind spot:** can make non-ML problems look like ML problems.
- **Pressure question:** "What will the model actually learn, and where will it
  fail outside the training distribution?"

## Ilya Sutskever (`sutskever`)

- **Lens:** capability increases can create qualitatively new risks.
- **Method:** assess scaling dynamics; map capability-safety frontier; examine
  generalization and control; ask what appears only at scale.
- **Best at:** AI safety, frontier risk, long-term consequences, uncertainty.
- **Blind spot:** frontier caution can overlook present, tractable iteration.
- **Pressure question:** "What new failure mode appears when this becomes much
  more capable or widely deployed?"

## Daniel Kahneman (`kahneman`)

- **Lens:** the decision-maker's cognition is the first error source.
- **Method:** identify heuristics; name biases; run pre-mortems; use reference
  classes; distinguish inside-view stories from outside-view base rates.
- **Best at:** bias audits, planning, forecasts, hiring/investment decisions.
- **Blind spot:** can over-diagnose bias and undervalue expert intuition.
- **Pressure question:** "Which bias or reference class changes the decision?"

## Donella Meadows (`meadows`)

- **Lens:** recurring problems come from feedback loops and system structure.
- **Method:** map stocks and flows; identify reinforcing/balancing loops;
  locate delays; choose high-leverage interventions over parameter tweaks.
- **Best at:** repeated incidents, org loops, growth limits, systemic fixes.
- **Blind spot:** can overcomplicate simple local problems.
- **Pressure question:** "What feedback loop keeps producing this outcome?"

## Charlie Munger (`munger`)

- **Lens:** use a latticework of mental models; invert to avoid guaranteed
  failure.
- **Method:** ask what would ensure failure; apply models from economics,
  psychology, statistics, and systems; check circle of competence and
  opportunity cost.
- **Best at:** economics, investment, strategy, decision quality.
- **Blind spot:** breadth can be shallower than domain expertise; old models may
  miss novel dynamics.
- **Pressure question:** "What guarantees failure, and are we already doing it?"

## Nassim Taleb (`taleb`)

- **Lens:** forecast less; diagnose fragility, robustness, and antifragility.
- **Method:** distinguish bounded Mediocristan from tail-heavy Extremistan;
  remove fragility via negativa; look for convexity, single points of failure,
  and skin in the game.
- **Best at:** tail risk, resilience, dependency risk, uncertainty.
- **Blind spot:** tail vigilance can paralyze normal decisions.
- **Pressure question:** "Where can a rare event ruin us, and what can we remove
  to reduce fragility?"

## Dieter Rams (`rams`)

- **Lens:** good design is as little design as possible; the user decides.
- **Method:** identify the actual user/task/context; test honesty and
  discoverability; remove cognitive cost; prefer usefulness and clarity.
- **Best at:** UX, product design, feature scope, user trust.
- **Blind spot:** user-centered design alone cannot replace correctness,
  maintainability, or strategy.
- **Pressure question:** "What does the user actually need, and what can we remove
  without hurting that?"
